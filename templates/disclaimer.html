Legal & Safety Disclaimer

Important: This is a prototype demonstration system. Please read these disclaimers carefully before deployment.

System Limitations

1. Not 100% Accurate

This system WILL make mistakes. Like all AI systems, it is not perfect:

  • False Positives: Innocent content may be incorrectly flagged as inappropriate (estimated 5-15% depending on content type)
  • False Negatives: Some inappropriate content may not be detected (estimated 3-8% based on testing)
  • Context Blindness: AI cannot understand context like humans can
  • Edge Cases: Unusual or ambiguous content may produce unpredictable results

Critical: Never rely solely on this system. Human oversight is essential.

2. Prototype Status

This is a demonstration prototype built for educational and evaluation purposes:

  • Not tested in real-world school environments at scale
  • May contain bugs or unexpected behaviors
  • Performance metrics based on controlled testing, not production use
  • Requires additional hardening for production deployment
  • Third-party dependencies (NudeNet, Sightengine) may change or become unavailable
3. Content Type Limitations

Known categories with higher error rates:

  • Beach/Pool Photos: Swimwear often triggers false positives (up to 15%)
  • Medical Content: Educational anatomy images frequently flagged incorrectly
  • Artistic Content: Figure drawings, sculptures, classical art misidentified
  • Sports/Athletics: Athletic wear can confuse detection algorithms
  • Cultural Dress: Traditional clothing from various cultures may be misinterpreted

Proper Use Guidelines

Appropriate Uses
  • As an assistive tool to help moderators identify potentially problematic content
  • To flag content for review, not to make final decisions
  • As part of a multi-layered safety approach
  • For educational purposes to demonstrate AI safety concepts
  • With proper staff training and oversight protocols
Inappropriate Uses
  • Sole basis for disciplinary action against students
  • Automatic content removal without human review
  • Mass surveillance of student content
  • Punishment-focused rather than education-focused implementation
  • Without clear policies and appeals processes
  • In lieu of proper staff training and digital citizenship education

Required Preconditions for Deployment

Before deploying this system, schools MUST have:

  1. Clear Policies: Written acceptable use policies explaining content rules
  2. Trained Staff: Administrators trained on AI limitations and review protocols
  3. Student Education: Programs teaching digital citizenship and safety
  4. Parent Communication: Parents informed about the system's use and limitations
  5. Appeal Process: Clear procedures for students to contest decisions
  6. Privacy Protections: Compliance with FERPA, COPPA, and state privacy laws
  7. Regular Audits: Ongoing monitoring for bias and accuracy
  8. Legal Review: Attorney review of implementation plans

Third-Party Services

This system uses third-party services that have their own terms and limitations:

  • NudeNet: Open-source model with its own accuracy limitations and license terms
  • Sightengine: Commercial API subject to their terms of service, pricing, and availability

Schools must separately evaluate and accept the terms of these third-party services.

Data & Privacy Disclaimers

  • While images are processed in memory, no system is 100% secure
  • Network interception or server compromise could expose image data
  • Metadata and hashes, while privacy-preserving, still constitute data collection
  • Third-party APIs (Sightengine) receive image data per their privacy policy
  • Schools must conduct their own privacy impact assessments

Ethical Considerations

Schools deploying this system should consider:

  • The psychological impact of surveillance on students
  • The potential for chilling effects on free expression
  • The burden placed on already-marginalized students
  • The balance between safety and privacy
  • The educational vs. punitive framing of the system

Recommended Alternatives & Supplements

This system works best when combined with:

  • Comprehensive digital citizenship curriculum
  • Open communication channels between students and trusted adults
  • Restorative justice practices rather than purely punitive responses
  • Ongoing staff training on child safety and appropriate responses
  • Student voice and participation in safety policy development
Best Practice: Technology should support, not replace, human relationships and education in creating safe school environments.
