User Education Mode

This page helps students understand digital safety and how content moderation works.

What Students Should Know

Why We Have Content Safety Systems

Schools use content safety tools to:

  • Keep everyone safe online
  • Prevent harmful content from spreading
  • Create a positive learning environment
  • Teach responsible digital citizenship

Important: These systems are here to help, not to punish. If something you post gets flagged, it doesn't automatically mean you did something wrong!

How the System Works

When you upload an image, here's what happens:

  1. AI Scans the Image: A computer program (AI) looks at the image to check if it might contain inappropriate content
  2. Confidence Score: The AI gives a confidence score (0-100%) about what it thinks it sees
  3. Three Possible Results:
    • Safe - No concerns detected
    • Suspect - AI isn't sure; needs human review
    • Harmful - AI detected concerning content; needs human review
  4. Human Review: If flagged, a school staff member reviews it to make the final decision
Why AI Isn't Perfect

AI systems can make mistakes! Here are some things that might get incorrectly flagged:

  • Beach or Pool Photos: Swimwear is normal at the beach but might confuse the AI
  • Medical or Science Content: Educational anatomy images might get flagged
  • Art Projects: Artistic representations of the human form might trigger false alarms
  • Sports Photos: Some athletic wear might be misunderstood

That's why we have human review! A real person will look at the context and make a fair decision.

Digital Safety Tips for Students

DO
  • Think before you share
  • Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable with my family seeing this?"
  • Report concerning content to a trusted adult
  • Respect others' privacy and consent
  • Ask permission before posting photos of others
  • Use appropriate content for school projects
DON'T
  • Share images without consent from people in them
  • Post or share inappropriate content
  • Assume AI is always right
  • Be afraid to ask for help if something gets flagged
  • Share private or personal images publicly
  • Use school accounts for personal photo sharing

What Happens If Your Content Gets Flagged?

  1. Don't Panic: The AI might be wrong! Remember, it's just a computer program.
  2. Human Review: A school staff member will look at it carefully.
  3. Context Matters: They'll consider why you posted it and what it's for.
  4. You Can Explain: You'll have a chance to explain the context.
  5. Appeal Process: If you disagree with a decision, you can appeal it.
  6. Learning Opportunity: Staff will help you understand what went wrong and how to improve.

Your Privacy Matters

How We Protect Your Privacy
  • Images are scanned but NOT permanently stored
  • Only the scan results are saved, not the actual picture
  • Your name is not attached to scan data
  • Only authorized staff can see flagged content
  • Everything is kept confidential

Questions & Support

If you have questions about content safety or think something was flagged incorrectly:

  • Talk to your teacher
  • Speak with a school counselor
  • Email the IT department
  • Your parents/guardians can also reach out

Remember: The goal is to keep everyone safe and help you learn, not to get you in trouble!

Digital Citizenship: Being a good digital citizen means being kind, respectful, and safe online. These tools help support that mission!
